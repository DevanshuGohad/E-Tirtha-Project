Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India

About the Temple

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the most famous Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India, it is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the holiest of Shiva temples.

Historical Significance

The temple has been referred to in Hindu scriptures for thousands of years and was built and re-built several times in history. The present structure was built in 1780 by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore.

Temple Architecture

The main temple is a quadrangle surrounded by smaller shrines. The main spire is covered with gold donated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh. The temple complex consists of several smaller temples and attracts millions of devotees annually.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (3:00 AM - 6:00 AM) is ideal for less crowd. The temple is most crowded during weekends and major festivals. Peak crowds during Mahashivratri (February/March), Shravan month (July/August), and during the wedding season (November-December).
