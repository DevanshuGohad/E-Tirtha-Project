Golden Temple

Amritsar, Punjab, India

About the Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest Gurdwara and the most important pilgrimage site of Sikhism. The temple is surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar (Pool of Nectar) and features stunning golden architecture.

Historical Significance

The temple was founded by Guru Ram Das in 1577 and completed by Guru Arjan Dev in 1604. The temple has been rebuilt several times after being damaged by various invasions. The current structure was rebuilt in 1764 by Jassa Singh Ahluwalia.

Temple Architecture

The temple features a unique blend of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. The main temple is built on a 67-square-foot platform in the center of the Amrit Sarovar. The temple's upper floors are covered with 750 kg of pure gold leaf, giving it its distinctive golden appearance.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (4:00 AM - 7:00 AM) is ideal for less crowd. The temple is most crowded during weekends and major festivals. Peak crowds during Gurpurab celebrations in November and New Year celebrations. Langar (community kitchen) is open 24/7, so you can plan your visit around meal times.
