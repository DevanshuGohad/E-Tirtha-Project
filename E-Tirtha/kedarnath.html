Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath, Uttarakhand, India

About the Temple

The Kedarnath Temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located in the Garhwal Himalayan range near the Mandakini river, it is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India.

Historical Significance

The temple is believed to have been built by the Pandavas and later renovated by Adi Shankaracharya. The temple has withstood several natural disasters and has been rebuilt multiple times. It is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites.

Temple Architecture

The temple is built of large, evenly cut grey slabs of stones. The temple has a Garbhagriha and a Mandapa. The temple's architecture is simple yet elegant, with intricate carvings on the walls. The temple is surrounded by snow-capped peaks.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (4:00 AM - 7:00 AM) is ideal for less crowd. The temple is most crowded during weekends and major festivals. Peak crowds during Shravan month (July/August) and during the Char Dham Yatra season. Temple remains closed during winter months (November to April).
