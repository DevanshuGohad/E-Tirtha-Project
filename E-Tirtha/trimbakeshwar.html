Trimbakeshwar Temple

Nashik, Maharashtra, India

About the Temple

The Trimbakeshwar Temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located in the town of Trimbak in Nashik district, it is the source of the Godavari River, the longest river in peninsular India.

Historical Significance

The temple has a rich history dating back to ancient times. It was built by the third Peshwa Balaji Bajirao (1740-1760) on the site of an older temple. The temple is mentioned in various Puranas and has been a significant pilgrimage site for centuries.

Temple Architecture

The temple is built in the Hemadpanthi style of architecture, featuring black stone construction. The main temple has three lingas representing Brahma, Vishnu, and Mahesh. The temple complex includes several smaller shrines and a sacred kund (water tank).

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (5:00 AM - 7:00 AM) is ideal for less crowd. The temple is most crowded during weekends and major festivals. Peak crowds during Mahashivratri (February/March) and Shravan month (July/August).
