Jagannath Temple

Puri, Odisha, India

About the Temple

The Jagannath Temple in Puri is one of the most revered temples in India, dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of Lord Krishna. It is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and is famous for its annual Rath Yatra festival.

Historical Significance

The temple was built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva of the Eastern Ganga dynasty. The temple is mentioned in various Puranas and has been a significant pilgrimage site for centuries. The temple's architecture reflects the Kalinga style.

Temple Architecture

The temple complex features a magnificent 65-meter high main tower and several smaller towers. The temple is built in the Kalinga style of architecture with intricate carvings and sculptures. The main temple has three deities: Lord Jagannath, his brother Balabhadra, and sister Subhadra.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (5:00 AM - 7:00 AM) for less crowd. Avoid weekends and major festivals like Rath Yatra for a more peaceful experience. The temple is most crowded during the summer months (April-July).
