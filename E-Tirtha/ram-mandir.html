Shri Ram Mandir

Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh, India

About the Temple

The Shri Ram Mandir in Ayodhya is a magnificent temple dedicated to Lord Rama, the seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu. This historic temple stands on the sacred site where Lord Rama is believed to have been born.

Historical Significance

The temple's construction began in 2020 after a historic Supreme Court verdict. The temple complex is designed in the Nagara style of architecture, featuring intricate carvings and sculptures depicting scenes from the Ramayana.

Temple Architecture

The main temple is a three-storied structure with 392 pillars and 44 doors. The temple is built using pink sandstone from Rajasthan and features elaborate carvings of Hindu deities and mythological scenes. The sanctum sanctorum houses the idol of Lord Rama.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (4:00 AM - 7:00 AM) is ideal for less crowd. The temple is most crowded during weekends and major festivals. Peak crowds during Ram Navami (March/April) and Diwali (October/November).
